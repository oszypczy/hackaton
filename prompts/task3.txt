# 2) Known watermark literature context relevant to this task
Task references and corpus indicate at least these watermark paradigms are relevant:
1) Kirchenbauer et al. 2023 (KGW / red-green token watermark)
- Hash-conditioned green/red partition over vocabulary
- Logit bias toward green tokens
- Classical detection via z-test:
  z = (n_green - gamma*T)/sqrt(T*gamma*(1-gamma))
- Typical defaults in known implementations: gamma=0.25, delta=2.0
- Known family variants include LeftHash and SelfHash-like seeding changes
- Important sensitivity: context width / edit fragility / repeated n-grams handling
2) Unigram-style watermarking (e.g., Zhao et al. 2024 framing)
- Static or less context-dependent green-list intuition
- Different robustness and detectability trade-offs than KGW
3) Semantic/sentence-level watermarking families (e.g., semantic-invariant ideas)
- Not purely token-id statistical
- More resilient to naive token-level detectors
- Require semantic-feature-aware detection logic
Adversarial pressure from literature:
- Black-box watermark stealing attacks (query-based reconstruction/spoof/scrub behavior)
- Paraphrase attacks (single-pass and recursive)
- Translation/rewriting attacks
- These attacks show watermark detectors can be brittle if over-specialized
Implication:
- A **Kirchenbauer-only detector is insufficient** for this benchmark objective.
- We need multi-branch + scheme-agnostic robustness.
